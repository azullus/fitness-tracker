1. **Week 1-2:** Continue current Metamucil dosage while implementing the fiber boost strategies below
2. **Week 3-4:** Reduce Metamucil to half dose (one serving per day instead of two, if currently taking twice daily)

- Switch regular pasta to whole grain or chickpea pasta when used (+3-5g fiber)
- Add 1/2 cup cooked lentils to salads or bowls twice weekly (+8g fiber per serving)
- Choose high-fiber crackers (5g+ per serving) instead of regular varieties

**Expected Outcome:** With these minor additions, daily fiber intake should reach 28-30g, making Metamucil unnecessary for regular bowel function. The natural food sources also provide additional micronutrients and phytochemicals that supplements lack.

**Note:** If constipation issues persist despite adequate fiber intake, consult a healthcare provider as other factors (hydration, stress, medications) may be involved.

## 7. Potential Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge 1: Difficulty Hitting Protein Targets
**Solution:**
- Keep pre-cooked chicken breast or hard-boiled eggs ready
- Use protein powder strategically (shakes, oats, smoothies)
- Choose protein-dense snacks (jerky, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese)

### Challenge 2: Time Constraints for Meal Prep
**Solution:**
- Batch cook proteins Sunday (all meats for week)
- Use Costco rotisserie chicken strategically (already in plan)
- Pre-chop vegetables in bulk
- Consider slow cooker/Instant Pot for hands-off cooking

### Challenge 3: Appetite Fluctuations
**Solution:**
- Liquid calories easier to consume (smoothies, shakes)
- Split large meals into smaller, more frequent meals
- Don't force food immediately post-workout if nauseous; wait 30-60 min

### Challenge 4: Budget Constraints
**Solution:**
- Buy meat on sale and freeze (already doing this well)
- Use cheaper cuts (chicken thighs > chicken breast, ground meats)
- Eggs are most cost-effective protein (~$0.20-30 per 6g protein)
- Store brand Greek yogurt vs name brand
- Bulk dried beans and lentils

### Challenge 5: Dining Out or Social Events
**Solution:**
- Choose protein-forward options (steaks, grilled chicken, fish)
- Ask for extra vegetables instead of fries
- Don't stress one meal; focus on daily/weekly averages
- Have protein shake before/after if restaurant options limited
